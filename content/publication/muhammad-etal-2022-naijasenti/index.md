abstract: Sentiment analysis is one of the most widely studied applications in NLP,
  but most work focuses on languages with large amounts of data. We introduce the
  first large-scale human-annotated Twitter sentiment dataset for the four most widely
  spoken languages in Nigeria---Hausa, Igbo, Nigerian-Pidgin, and Yorùbá---consisting
  of around 30,000 annotated tweets per language, including a significant fraction
  of code-mixed tweets. We propose text collection, filtering, processing and labeling
  methods that enable us to create datasets for these low-resource languages. We evaluate
  a range of pre-trained models and transfer strategies on the dataset. We find that
  language-specific models and language-adaptive fine-tuning generally perform best.
  We release the datasets, trained models, sentiment lexicons, and code to incentivize
  research on sentiment analysis in under-represented languages.
